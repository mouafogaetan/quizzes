SECTION C: READING AND COMPREHENSION (10 points)

THE BENEFITS OF A HOBBY

Sarah (not her real name) is a young fashion designer who draws inspiration from traditional West African fabrics and clothing, as well as from the fashions of London. She has created a label that produces modern interpretations of the "kaba", which is the traditional garment from Cameroon's littoral region.

For Sarah, this whole adventure started as a hobby. She says; "I have been nurturing my dream, which started as a hobby, for a long time. I always saw my love for designing as a hobby, and it never occurred to me that it could be a career. Indeed, no one in my family would have considered this as a suitable career for a well-educated girl from a respectable family! As expected by my family, I studied at university and successfully graduated with two degrees.

"But while I was studying, I was expanding my skills in design and dressmaking and exploring my passion for fashion. Then one day, while I was at work, things changed for me. I was wearing one of my own dresses, made from hand-woven fabric. A customer approached me and asked where she could buy a dress like mine, and then she ordered one. My hobby was about to become a career!"

Answer the following questions.

a) What is Sarah's job? (2 points)

b) What does she draw inspiration from? (2 points)

c) Which type of family is Sarah from? From a respectable family or poor family? (2 points)

d) What else was she doing while studying? (2 points)

e) Which career would you like to pursue, and why? (2 points)
