SECTION ONE: GRAMMAR (10 MARKS)

I. Complete the following sentences with the correct form of the words in brackets. (5 marks)

  1. Students, it's high time you __________ for your final examinations. (to prepare)
  2. It is __________ to use mobile phones during classes. (to prohibit)
  3. Leaving trash in public spaces is one of the __________ habits. (bad)
  4. The __________ of the Sanaga River is approximately 800 m. (wide)
  5. Our neighborhood is __________ than it was five years ago. (clean)

II. Use the correct form of the verbs in brackets to complete the following sentences (5 marks)

  1. If she __________ the opportunity, she would study abroad. (to have)
  2. By next year, I __________ my university degree. (to complete)
  3. Have you ever __________ to Yaoundé? (to go)
  4. Many students __________ interested in science subjects nowadays. (to be)
  5. Had they informed me earlier, I __________ the meeting. (not to miss)

SECTION TWO: VOCABULARY (10 MARKS)

I. Choose a suitable word from the brackets to fill in the gaps. (5 marks)

  1. I often forget appointments. Could you please __________ me tomorrow? (remind, remember, recall)
  2. It's difficult to __________ extreme heat without air conditioning. (tolerate, accept, support)
  3. We obtain palm oil from palm __________. (nuts, fruits, seeds)
  4. I haven't seen __________ in the library this morning. (someone, anyone, no one)
  5. Next year, we will celebrate the __________ anniversary of our school. (sixtieth, sixty, sixteenth)

II. Complete this passage with a suitable word or phrase of your own. (5 marks)

  1. Roger Milla earned his __________ as a professional footballer.
  2. My uncle is a blacksmith. He makes __________ like machetes and knives.
  3. We always use __________ before names of specific people or places.
  4. When a man and woman get married, she becomes his __________ and he becomes her husband.
  5. For any emergency, you can dial the __________ number 117 to contact the police.

SECTION THREE: READING COMPREHENSION (10 MARKS)

Read the following passage and answer the questions that follow:

The School Principal,
Distinguished teachers,
Members of the Parent-Teacher Association,
Dear students,
Ladies and gentlemen.

[...] It is with great honor that I address you today as the head of the student council on this special occasion of our school's 25th anniversary celebration. This year's theme is "Youth, Digital Innovation and National Development".

The youth represent the future of our nation - those who have transitioned from childhood but have not yet reached full adulthood. Digital innovation refers to the creative use of technology to solve problems and improve lives. When we talk about national development, we mean the progress of our country in economic, social, and technological aspects.

By embracing technology responsibly, by developing digital skills, and by using these skills to address local challenges, we contribute to our nation's growth. [...]

Digital literacy and national development are therefore crucial areas where we must focus our efforts to help achieve Cameroon's Vision 2035. [...]

Adapted from a student council leader's address, 2022

Questions:

  1. How does the speaker define "youth" in this context?
  2. What is meant by "digital innovation" according to the passage?
  3. Besides digital innovation, suggest two other areas that could contribute to national development.
  4. What important national event is celebrated on May 20th each year in Cameroon?
  5. What factors might hinder digital innovation in our communities?
