SECTION A: GRAMMAR (10 points)

Exercise A: Dialogue completion (5 points)

Mary: Good morning Peter. How are you doing?

Peter: Good morning dear. I am fine and you?

Mary: I'm fine too, thanks. Please, I would like to inquire how you went about applying for a passport.

Peter: Well! Last week, I went (to go) to the Embassy to start my procedure but the consular officer told (to tell) me that I first needed to register online.

Mary: Ok. I see. You finally registered online? (Question tag)

Peter: Yes of course, although (put conjunction) it was not easy. I printed my form at (put preposition) 8 p.m. as there was a power cut. Now, I can return there to get an appointment.

Mary: Good luck my friend. Thanks for the requirement procedure.

Teacher's Notes:
  • "went" - past simple of "to go"
  • "told" - past simple of "to tell"
  • "although" - conjunction showing contrast
  • "at" - preposition used with specific times

Exercise B: Sentence completion (5 points)

i. He returned home at the end of day (question tag) didn't he?

Explanation: We use the negative question tag "didn't he?" because the main statement is positive.

ii. What I cannot understand is how you could turn down this golden opportunity. (off, down, up)

Explanation: "Turn down" means to reject or refuse an offer.

iii. By the time Shakespeare died, he had written many books. (to write)

Explanation: Past perfect tense is used for an action completed before another past action.

iv. Children are fond of playing during their free time, rather than resting even after a day of work. (fond, fun, fon)

Explanation: "Fond of" means having a liking for something.

v. English / value / French / and / have / equal. (use "should" to form a question with the jumbled words) Should English and French have equal value?

Explanation: The correct question structure with "should" and proper word order.

SECTION B: VOCABULARY (10 points)

A. Noun formation (5 points)

1. The interview was a bit scary.

2. The impression was not at all good.

3. The interviewer expressed satisfaction at the jobseeker's presence.

4. The interviewer asked me to make my presentation.

5. The arrival of the manager marked the end of the interview.

Word Formation Rules:
  • interview (verb) → interview (noun)
  • impress (verb) → impression (noun)
  • satisfy (verb) → satisfaction (noun)
  • present (verb) → presentation (noun)
  • arrive (verb) → arrival (noun)

B. Paragraph completion (5 points)

When you go for a job interview, remember that first impressions count.

Dress smartly/professionally/appropriately, but not as if you're going to a party. The interviewer will ask questions about your experience and qualifications/background/skills. Be honest/truthful/sincere in your answers because dishonesty never pays.

Acceptable Answers:
  • impressions (most appropriate)
  • smartly, professionally, appropriately (all acceptable)
  • interviewer, employer, panel (all acceptable)
  • qualifications, background, skills, education (all acceptable)
  • honest, truthful, sincere (all acceptable)
SECTION C: READING AND COMPREHENSION (10 points)

THE BENEFITS OF A HOBBY

Answers to comprehension questions:

a) What is Sarah's job? She is a fashion designer. (2 points)

b) What does she draw inspiration from? She draws inspiration from traditional West African fabrics and clothing, as well as from the fashions of London. (2 points)

c) Which type of family is Sarah from? From a respectable family or poor family? She is from a respectable family. (2 points)

d) What else was she doing while studying? While studying, she was expanding her skills in design and dressmaking and exploring her passion for fashion. (2 points)

e) Which career would you like to pursue, and why? (Sample answer) I would like to pursue a career in medicine because I want to help people and make a difference in their lives. I find the human body fascinating and enjoy solving complex problems. (2 points)

Grading Guidelines for Question e:
  • 2 points: Clear career choice with well-explained reasons
  • 1 point: Career mentioned but reasons poorly explained
  • 0 points: No answer or irrelevant response
SECTION D: COMPOSITION WRITING (5 points)

Application Letter Evaluation Criteria:

Sample Application Letter:

NGOUMOU David
P.O. Box 321
Yaoundé
[Date]

The General Manager
XYZ Trading
P.O. Box 456
Douala

Dear Sir/Madam,

I am writing to apply for the position advertised in [source of advertisement] on [date]. I believe my qualifications and experience make me a strong candidate for this role.

I hold a [degree/diploma] in [field] from [institution] and have [number] years of experience in [relevant field]. In my previous role at [previous company], I was responsible for [key responsibilities] which resulted in [positive outcomes].

Some of the key attributes I would bring to your company include:

  • Strong communication and interpersonal skills
  • Ability to work effectively in team environments
  • Problem-solving capabilities and attention to detail
  • Adaptability and eagerness to learn new skills

I am enthusiastic about the opportunity to contribute to XYZ Trading and believe my skills and experience would be an asset to your company.

Thank you for considering my application. I have attached my CV for your review and look forward to the possibility of discussing my qualifications further in an interview.

Yours faithfully,
NGOUMOU David

Grading Rubric for Composition:
  • Format (1 point): Proper letter structure (sender's address, date, recipient's address, salutation, closing)
  • Content (2 points): Inclusion of qualifications, work experience, and personal attributes
  • Language (1 point): Correct grammar, spelling, and punctuation
  • Coherence (1 point): Logical flow and organization of ideas
