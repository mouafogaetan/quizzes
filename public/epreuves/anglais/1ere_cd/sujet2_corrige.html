Task 1: Dialogue Completion (5 marks)

had taken (1 mark)

Doctor: You sleep under a treated mosquito net, __________?

don't you (1 mark)

Doctor: I see from the test results that you have __________ parasites and amoeba in your blood and stool samples.

many (1 mark)

Doctor: __________ you boil the water you drink?

Do (1 mark)

Doctor: You __________ boil any water from questionable sources.

must (1 mark)

Task 2: Sentence Completion (5 marks)

1) "My friends and I participated in the youth week activities yesterday," ZOKO told his teacher. (Reported speech)

ZOKO told his teacher that he and his friends had participated in the youth week activities the day before. (1 mark)

2) You should go on a diet or start working out. (Continue the sentence using "either...or")

Either you should go on a diet or start working out. (1 mark)

3) She __________ to Goree Island yet. (Present perfect tense)

has not been (1 mark)

4) If she were elected president, she __________ corporal punishment illegal. (Type 2 conditional)

would make (1 mark)

5) It costs about 800 CFA to get a haircut at the barbershop. (Ask the question)

How much does it cost to get a haircut at the barbershop? (1 mark)

Part Two: Assessment of Competence (20 marks)
Section C: Reading Comprehension (10 marks)

1) According to the text, who promotes ethnic favoritism?

Political elites (1 mark)

2) Besides conflict, what negative effects has tribalism brought according to the text?

Difficulty combating corruption and employment/contract bias (2 marks - 1 mark each)

3) Why is combating corruption difficult as stated in the text?

Because there's a tradition that discourages exposing or prosecuting fellow ethnic members for corrupt practices (2 marks)

4) How do guilty individuals escape justice according to the passage?

They invoke ethnic solidarity/sentiment to avoid accountability (1 mark)

5) Are public contracts awarded based on merit and excellence? True or false? Justify your answer.

False. The text states that merit and excellence are sacrificed for ethnic considerations (1 mark)

6) State two significant issues caused by tribalism according to the text.

Any two of: difficulty fighting corruption, employment/contract bias, culture of distrust among ethnic groups (2 marks - 1 mark each)

7) Despite tribalism, national integration is occurring through four ways. Name them.

Intermarriage, shared religion, commerce, and internal migration (2 marks - 0.5 mark each)

8) Do you think redrawing national borders along ethnic lines would solve problems of tribalism and corruption in your country? Explain.

(Open-ended but should demonstrate understanding of the text) Sample answer: No, because the text suggests that corruption and tyranny are individual traits, not ethnic characteristics, and that solutions lie in good governance, justice, and equal opportunity rather than ethnic separation. (2 marks - 1 for position, 1 for justification)
