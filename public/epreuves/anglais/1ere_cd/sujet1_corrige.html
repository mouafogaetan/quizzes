SECTION A: GRAMMAR (10 points)

I) Complete the dialogue:

Sarah: Mom, may I please join the environmental club at school?
Mother: I'm sorry, but I cannot allow you to do that right now. Your academic performance must improve first.
Sarah: But mom, many of my friends are already members.
Mother: I understand, Sarah. However, you must focus more on your studies given your recent grades. When your results improve, I'll reconsider.
Sarah: I keep forgetting, mom. I hope my performance improves soon.

Points clés: utilisation des modaux (cannot, must), pronom possessif (my), conjugaison verbale (improves).

5 points

II) Choose the correct answer:

1) David and Michael are brothers but they rarely help each other.
"Each other" est utilisé pour deux personnes qui effectuent une action réciproque.

2) Lisa is very reserved. She doesn't socialize with most of her colleagues.
"Most of" indique la majorité mais pas la totalité.

3) Teenagers should avoid both drugs and alcohol.
"Both...and" est la construction correcte pour indiquer deux éléments.

4) I have been studying French since 2018.
"Since" est utilisé avec un point précis dans le temps.

5) My father felt uncomfortable discussing relationships with me.
L'adjectif "uncomfortable" décrit l'état du père.

5 points

SECTION C: READING COMPREHENSION (10 points)

Réponses aux questions:

1) Anyone can experience adjustment difficulties in a new country, regardless of their origin or destination.
Le texte précise que "This can affect anyone, no matter where you come from or where you go".

2) Two factors are: being in a new environment with different customs, and feeling homesick missing family and friends.
Le texte mentionne "the shock of being in a new environment" et "if you feel homesick and miss your family and friends".

3) When feeling homesick, it's advisable to keep in touch with home through technology but not travel home too often, and make friends with other international students.
Le texte recommande: "make sure you keep in touch with home. Phone or skype home regularly... But don't travel home too often... Make friends with fellow foreign students".

4) Food can be a problem because it may taste very different from what students are used to in their home country.
Le texte indique: "Another problem area for overseas students is food. It may taste very different from what you are used to."

5) One can help a foreign classmate by befriending them and helping them learn about local culture and customs.
Le texte suggère: "make friends with as many local students as you can. They will help you learn about British culture."

10 points (2 points par réponse correcte)
SECTION D: COMPOSITION (10 points)

Critères d'évaluation:

  • Respect de la longueur demandée (250-300 mots): 2 points
  • Structure et organisation: 2 points
  • Exactitude grammaticale: 2 points
  • Richeses du vocabulaire: 2 points
  • Respect des consignes spécifiques: 2 points

Exemple de lettre (Topic 1):

Alex Brown P.O. Box 123 Harmony City October 29, 2023 Dear Taylor, I am writing this letter with a heavy heart, hoping to mend our friendship that means so much to me. I deeply regret our argument last week and want to sincerely apologize for my part in it. I know I was wrong to dismiss your concerns about the group project. I should have listened more carefully to your suggestions instead of being so defensive. My behavior was unacceptable, and I understand why you felt hurt and frustrated. I want to assure you that I am committed to resolving this matter. I promise to be more considerate of your opinions in the future and to communicate better when we have disagreements. I value our friendship too much to let this incident ruin it. Please know that I am willing to do whatever it takes to regain your trust. I hope we can meet soon to talk things over properly. Your friend, Alex Word count: 158

Exemple d'article (Topic 2):

The Importance of Living Together in Harmony By Jordan Lee In our diverse community, misunderstandings and discrimination continue to create divisions among us. It is crucial to address these issues and promote peaceful coexistence. Three main causes of discrimination include: first, lack of education about different cultures; second, preconceived stereotypes passed down through generations; and third, fear of the unknown or anything different from our own experiences. The consequences of these problems are severe: they create social divisions, limit economic opportunities for marginalized groups, and perpetuate cycles of hatred and violence. To foster better living together, we can: first, organize community events that celebrate cultural diversity; second, implement educational programs in schools about different traditions; and third, encourage open dialogues between different community groups. The advantages of living together in harmony are numerous: it creates a more peaceful society, enriches our cultural experiences through exchange, and promotes economic development by utilizing everyone's talents. Let us all commit to building a community where everyone feels valued and respected. Word count: 165
