Personal and Family Identity

Exploring who we are and where we come from

Introduction

Welcome to the chapter on Personal and Family Identity. In this unit, we will explore how to introduce ourselves and others, discuss family structures and relationships, and practice filling out important identity documents. These skills are fundamental to building connections and understanding our place in the world.

Chapter Objectives

Introduce Yourself and Others

Learn to share basic information such as name, age, origin, and background.

Talk About Family

Describe family structure, roles, and responsibilities within a family.

Complete Identity Forms

Practice filling out various identity forms with confidence.

Essential Vocabulary

Personal Information
  • Name, surname, given name
  • Age, date of birth
  • Nationality, origin
  • Occupation, profession
  • Address, contact information
Family Members
  • Parents, siblings
  • Relatives, extended family
  • Spouse, partner
  • Children, offspring
  • Generations: grandparents, grandchildren

Linguistic Focus

Simple Present Tense

Used for habits, general truths, and permanent situations:

  • I live in Paris.
  • She works as a teacher.
  • We have two children.
Possessive Adjectives

Show ownership or relationship:

  • My name is Anna.
  • This is our house.
  • That is their dog.
  • He is her brother.
Question Forms

Asking for personal information:

  • What is your name?
  • How old are you?
  • Where are you from?
  • Who are your family members?

  1. My name is __________.

  3. I am from __________.
  4. My family consists of __________.

Final Task: Short Biography

For your final task, you will write and present a short biography about yourself or a family member.

Guidelines:
  • Include basic personal information (name, age, origin)
  • Describe your family background
  • Mention important life events or achievements
  • Use appropriate grammar structures learned in this chapter
  • Your biography should be 150-200 words
Example Outline:

My name is [Your Name]. I was born on [Date] in [Place]. I currently live in [City, Country]. I have [number] siblings. My father works as a [occupation] and my mother is a [occupation].

In my family, we value [value] and enjoy [activity] together. I remember when [memory]. This experience taught me [lesson].

In the future, I hope to [aspiration]. My family supports me in this goal by [support].

Cultural Note
Family Structures Around the World

Family structures vary significantly across cultures. In some societies, extended families living together are common, while in others, nuclear families are the norm. When discussing family, be mindful that not everyone has the same family structure, and some people may have chosen families or non-traditional arrangements.

Example Biography

Maria González

My name is Maria González. I am 28 years old and I was born in Barcelona, Spain. I currently live in Madrid where I work as a graphic designer. I come from a large family - I have two older sisters and one younger brother.

My father is a retired teacher and my mother works as a nurse. In our family, we value education and creativity. Every Sunday, we have a large family lunch where we share stories and enjoy traditional Spanish dishes together.

One of my fondest memories is our annual summer trip to the countryside to visit my grandparents. Those summers taught me the importance of family bonds and connecting with our roots.

In the future, I hope to start my own design studio. My family supports me in this goal by encouraging my creativity and offering advice when I need it. I'm grateful for the strong foundation my family has given me.
