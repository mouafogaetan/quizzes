Formal Letter and Speech Writing

Maîtrisez l'art de l'écriture formelle pour les lettres et les discours

Objectifs d'apprentissage

  • Comprendre la structure et les conventions des lettres formelles
  • Maîtriser le langage formel approprié pour différentes situations
  • Apprendre à structurer un discours efficace avec une ouverture engageante et une conclusion forte
  • Utiliser des techniques rhétoriques pour rendre un discours persuasif
  • Identifier et éviter les erreurs courantes dans l'écriture formelle

Exemple de lettre formelle

123 School Road Paris, 75001 France October 15, 2023 The Mayor City Hall Paris, 75001 Dear Mr. Mayor, Subject: Complaint about noise pollution in the city center I am writing to bring to your attention the ongoing issue of excessive noise pollution in the city center, particularly during nighttime hours. For the past three months, residents in the area have been disturbed by loud music from establishments operating after permitted hours. This situation has made it difficult for many of us to sleep properly and has affected our quality of life. I would like to suggest stricter enforcement of noise regulations and earlier closing times for entertainment venues in residential areas. Thank you for considering my concerns. I look forward to your response. Yours sincerely, Marie Lambert

Élément Description Exemple

Techniques rhétoriques efficaces

Rule of Three

Présenter les idées par trois pour plus d'impact

"Education, practice, and perseverance - these are the keys to digital mastery."

Questions rhétoriques

Poser des questions pour engager l'audience

"How many of you use a computer daily? How would your life change without this technology?"

Anaphore

Répétition en début de phrase pour emphasis

"We need computers to learn. We need computers to work. We need computers to connect."

Appel à l'action

Encourager l'audience à agir

"Let us all commit to improving our digital skills starting today."

Exemple de discours

Dear students, teachers, and honored guests, Have you ever imagined a day without computers? No social media, no online classes, no digital entertainment? It's difficult, isn't it? This shows how integral technology has become to our lives. Today, on School Computer Literacy Day, I want to emphasize why digital skills are no longer optional but essential. First, computer literacy opens doors to future employment. Nearly 90% of jobs now require basic digital skills. Second, it empowers us to navigate the modern world safely and effectively. And third, it provides tools for creativity and innovation that previous generations could only dream of. But how can we improve our digital literacy? Start with the basics: learn to type properly, understand how to research online, and practice using common software. Don't be afraid to experiment and make mistakes—that's how we learn. In conclusion, I urge each of you to take advantage of the resources available to improve your computer skills. Our future depends on it. Thank you.

Exercice 3 - Exemple de réponse (Lettre)
123 Main Street Paris, 75002 France October 15, 2023 The Manager ElectroWorld Store 456 Shopping Avenue Paris, 75003 Dear Sir/Madam, Subject: Complaint regarding defective computer purchase I am writing to express my dissatisfaction with a computer I purchased from your store on October 10, 2023. The model is a TechMaster Pro Laptop (serial number: TM-123456). Despite following all instructions carefully, the device has been experiencing frequent crashes and overheating issues since the first day of use. I have attempted to troubleshoot these problems without success. As the product is still under warranty, I would like to request either a full refund or an exchange for a functioning device. I have attached a copy of the receipt and warranty documentation for your reference. I would appreciate your prompt attention to this matter. Please contact me at your earliest convenience to discuss the resolution. Thank you for your assistance. Yours sincerely, Jean Moreau

Conseils pour l'examen

  • Respectez la structure : Une lettre formelle doit suivre le format approprié avec tous les éléments requis.
  • Ton formel : Évitez les contractions (utilisez "do not" au lieu de "don't") et le langage familier.
  • Clarté et concision : Allez droit au but avec des phrases courtes et des paragraphes bien structurés.
  • Ponctuation : Dans les lettres formelles, utilisez une ponctuation complète (Dear Mr. Smith,:).
  • Pratiquez : Entraînez-vous à écrire dans les temps impartis pour gérer efficacement le jour de l'examen.

Erreurs fréquentes

  • Confondre "Yours sincerely" (quand on connaît le nom du destinataire) et "Yours faithfully" (quand on ne le connaît pas)
  • Oublier d'inclure l'objet de la lettre (Subject line)
  • Utiliser un langage trop direct ou impoli
  • Négliger la structure des paragraphes (un par idée principale)
  • Faire des phrases trop longues et complexes qui nuisent à la clarté
  • Oublier de relire et de corriger les fautes d'orthographe et de grammaire

Stratégies pour réussir

  • Analysez le sujet : Identifiez le type de document demandé, le public cible et le but de l'écriture.
  • Planifiez : Passez 5 minutes à faire un plan avant de commencer à rédiger.
  • Variez le vocabulaire : Utilisez un registre formel et évitez les répétitions.
  • Soignez la conclusion : Terminez par une phrase forte qui résume votre message principal.
  • Gérez votre temps : Réservez au moins 5 minutes pour vous relire et corriger les erreurs.

Expressions utiles

Pour les lettres
  • I am writing to express my concern about...
  • I would be grateful if you could...
  • I look forward to your response.
  • Thank you for your attention to this matter.
Pour les discours
  • Let me begin by asking you...
  • There are three key points to consider...
  • In conclusion, I would like to emphasize...
  • Thank you for your kind attention.
