Chapitre 7: Transformation Exercises

Ce chapitre couvre les techniques essentielles pour transformer des phrases tout en conservant leur sens original, une compétence cruciale pour les examens d'anglais.

Objectifs d'Apprentissage

À la fin de cette leçon, vous serez capable de:

  • Convertir des phrases de la voix active à la voix passive dans tous les temps
  • Transformer le discours direct en discours indirect avec les changements appropriés
  • Modifier le début des phrases en utilisant différentes structures grammaticales
  • Former correctement le pluriel des noms irréguliers

1. Voix Active à Passive

Règles de transformation

Pour transformer une phrase active en passive :

  1. L'objet de la phrase active devient le sujet de la phrase passive
  2. On ajoute la forme appropriée de "be" correspondant au temps du verbe actif
  3. Le verbe principal est converti au participe passé
  4. Le sujet de la phrase active devient complément d'agent introduit par "by" (optionnel)
Temps Actif Passif
Présent simple She writes a letter. A letter is written by her.
Présent continu She is writing a letter. A letter is being written by her.
Passé simple She wrote a letter. A letter was written by her.
Passé continu She was writing a letter. A letter was being written by her.
Futur simple She will write a letter. A letter will be written by her.
Present perfect She has written a letter. A letter has been written by her.
Modaux She can write a letter. A letter can be written by her.
Exercices: Active to Passive

Transformez les phrases suivantes en utilisant la voix passive:

  1. The chef prepares the meal every day.
    (Begin with: The meal...)
  2. They are building a new hospital near my house.
    (Begin with: A new hospital...)
  3. Someone stole my bicycle yesterday.
    (Begin with: My bicycle...)

→ Afficher/Masquer les réponses

  1. The meal is prepared by the chef every day.
  2. A new hospital is being built near my house.
  3. My bicycle was stolen yesterday.

2. Discours Direct à Indirect

Règles de transformation

Lorsqu'on transforme le discours direct en discours indirect, plusieurs changements sont nécessaires:

Élément Discours direct Discours indirect
Temps Present simple →
Present continuous →
Will →
Can →		 Past simple
Past continuous
Would
Could
Pronoms I, we, my, our He/she, they, his/her, their
Adverbes de temps now, today, tomorrow, yesterday then, that day, the next day, the day before
Adverbes de lieu here, this, these there, that, those

Exemples:

  • Direct: "I love this book," she said.
  • Indirect: She said that she loved that book.
  • Direct: "We will meet tomorrow," he said.
  • Indirect: He said that they would meet the next day.
Exercices: Direct to Indirect Speech

Transformez les phrases suivantes en discours indirect:

  1. "I am studying for my exam," she said.
  2. "We have finished our project," they announced.
  3. "I will call you tomorrow," he promised.

→ Afficher/Masquer les réponses

  1. She said that she was studying for her exam.
  2. They announced that they had finished their project.
  3. He promised that he would call me the next day.

3. Changer le Début des Phrases

Structures alternatives

Il existe plusieurs façons d'exprimer la même idée en modifiant le début de la phrase:

Structure originale Transformation Exemple
Although/Even though In spite of/Despite Although it was raining → Despite the rain
Because/Since Because of/Due to Because he was tired → Because of his tiredness
To-infinitive Gerund To save money → Saving money
If-condition Unless If you don't study → Unless you study

Exemples supplémentaires:

  • Although he was rich → In spite of being rich/In spite of his wealth
  • Because she was ill → Because of her illness
  • To improve your English → Improving your English
Exercices: Changing Sentence Beginnings

Réécrivez les phrases suivantes en commençant par les mots indiqués:

  1. Although he was tired, he finished the work.
    (Begin with: In spite of...)
  2. Because the weather was bad, the flight was delayed.
    (Begin with: Due to...)
  3. To pass the exam requires diligent study.
    (Begin with: Passing...)

→ Afficher/Masquer les réponses

  1. In spite of his tiredness, he finished the work.
  2. Due to the bad weather, the flight was delayed.
  3. Passing the exam requires diligent study.

4. Pluriels Irréguliers

Règles de formation du pluriel

Contrairement à la plupart des noms qui forment leur pluriel en ajoutant -s, certains noms ont des pluriels irréguliers:

Règle Singulier Pluriel
Changement de voyelle man, woman, foot, tooth men, women, feet, teeth
Ajout de -en child, ox children, oxen
Mots d'origine étrangère criterion, phenomenon, analysis criteria, phenomena, analyses
Formes identiques sheep, deer, series sheep, deer, series
Mots se terminant par -f/-fe knife, leaf, wife knives, leaves, wives
Exercices: Pluralisation

Mettez les mots entre parenthèses au pluriel dans les phrases suivantes:

  1. The (child) are playing in the park.
  2. Scientists observed unusual (phenomenon) during their research.
  3. We need to sharpen these (knife).

→ Afficher/Masquer les réponses

  1. The children are playing in the park.
  2. Scientists observed unusual phenomena during their research.
  3. We need to sharpen these knives.

Conseils pour l'Examen & Erreurs Fréquentes

Conseils importants:
  • Lisez attentivement les instructions et respectez le nombre de mots demandé
  • Vérifiez que la phrase transformée a exactement le même sens que l'originale
  • Portez une attention particulière aux temps verbaux et à la concordance des temps
  • N'oubliez pas de changer les références de temps (today → that day) et de lieu (here → there)
Erreurs fréquentes à éviter:
  • Oublier de changer le temps verbal dans le discours indirect
  • Utiliser la mauvaise forme de "be" dans la voix passive
  • Confondre "although" (suivi d'une phrase) et "in spite of" (suivi d'un nom ou d'un gérondif)
  • Appliquer les règles de pluriel régulier aux noms irréguliers (childs au lieu de children)
  • Négliger les changements nécessaires des pronoms et adverbes

Exercice de Synthèse

Transformez les phrases suivantes comme indiqué:

  1. "I can't attend the meeting tomorrow," she said. (Transformez en discours indirect)
  2. The company will launch a new product next month. (Transformez à la voix passive)
  3. Although it was expensive, we decided to buy the car. (Commencez par "In spite of...")
  4. The (criterion) for evaluation are clearly stated. (Mettez le mot entre parenthèses au pluriel)
  5. Someone has stolen my wallet. (Transformez à la voix passive)

→ Afficher/Masquer les réponses

  1. She said that she couldn't attend the meeting the next day.
  2. A new product will be launched by the company next month.
  3. In spite of the expense, we decided to buy the car.
  4. The criteria for evaluation are clearly stated.
  5. My wallet has been stolen.

Pratiquez régulièrement ces transformations pour maîtriser ces techniques essentielles à l'examen.
