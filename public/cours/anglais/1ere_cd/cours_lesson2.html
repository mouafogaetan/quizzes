Chapitre 2: Structures de Phrases Complexes

Objectifs d'Apprentissage

  • Maîtriser l'utilisation des propositions relatives (définies et non-définies)
  • Comprendre et appliquer correctement les trois types de phrases conditionnelles
  • Utiliser diverses conjonctions pour créer des phrases complexes
  • Combiner des phrases simples en structures complexes et sophistiquées

1. Propositions Relatives

Les propositions relatives fournissent des informations supplémentaires sur un nom. Elles peuvent être définies (essentielles) ou non-définies (non-essentielles).

Pronoms relatifs et leur usage

Pronom Usage Exemple
Who Personnes (sujet) The woman who called you is my boss.
Whom Personnes (objet - formel) The man whom you met is our director.
Which Choses et animaux The book, which is on the table, is mine.
That Personnes ou choses (dans les relatives définies) The car that I bought is very efficient.
Whose Possession (personnes ou choses) The student whose project won is very talented.

Relatives Définies vs Non-Définies

Type Fonction Exemple
Définie Essentielle au sens de la phrase, pas de virgules Students who study hard usually succeed.
Non-définie Information supplémentaire, entre virgules My brother, who lives in Paris, is visiting us.

Exercice: Propositions Relatives

Combine les paires de phrases en utilisant une proposition relative.

  1. I talked to the woman. She owns the hotel.
  2. The book is very interesting. You lent it to me.
  3. That's the artist. His paintings are famous worldwide.

Voir les réponses suggérées

  1. I talked to the woman who owns the hotel.
  2. The book that you lent to me is very interesting.
  3. That's the artist whose paintings are famous worldwide.

2. Phrases Conditionnelles

Les phrases conditionnelles expriment une condition et son résultat. Elles sont divisées en trois types principaux.

Types de conditionnelles

Type Usage Structure Exemple
Type 1 Situations réelles ou possibles If + Present Simple, will + base verb If it rains, we will cancel the picnic.
Type 2 Situations hypothétiques ou improbables If + Past Simple, would + base verb If I won the lottery, I would travel the world.
Type 3 Situations passées et hypothétiques If + Past Perfect, would have + past participle If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam.

Alternatives à "if"

Mot Usage Exemple
Unless Si... ne pas (sauf si) Unless you hurry, you'll miss the train.
Provided that À condition que I'll help you provided that you try first.
As long as Tant que You can borrow my car as long as you drive carefully.

Exercice: Phrases Conditionnelles

Complétez les phrases avec la forme verbale correcte.

  1. If she __________ (study) harder, she would have passed the exam.
  2. Unless you __________ (apologize), she won't forgive you.
  3. If I see him, I __________ (give) him the message.

Voir les réponses suggérées

  1. If she had studied harder, she would have passed the exam.
  2. Unless you apologize, she won't forgive you.
  3. If I see him, I will give him the message.

3. Conjonctions

Les conjonctions relient des mots, des phrases ou des clauses. Elles aident à créer des phrases plus complexes et sophistiquées.

Conjonctions courantes et leur usage

Conjonction Usage Exemple
Although/Even though Bien que Although it was raining, we went for a walk.
Despite/In spite of Malgré Despite the rain, we went for a walk.
Not only... but also Non seulement... mais aussi She is not only intelligent but also hardworking.
Whereas/While Tandis que/alors que I prefer tea while my brother prefers coffee.

Exercice: Conjonctions

Combine les deux phrases en utilisant la conjonction indiquée.

  1. It was raining. We decided to go out. (although)
  2. She is a talented singer. She is a great dancer. (not only... but also)
  3. He failed the exam. He had studied very hard. (despite)

Voir les réponses suggérées

  1. Although it was raining, we decided to go out.
  2. She is not only a talented singer but also a great dancer.
  3. Despite having studied very hard, he failed the exam.

Exercice de Synthèse

Combine les phrases simples suivantes en une seule phrase complexe en utilisant le mot ou la structure indiquée.

  1. Join using a relative clause:
    The man is my uncle. He is wearing a blue shirt.
  2. Join using a conditional (type 2):
    I don't have enough money. I can't buy that car.
  3. Join using "not only... but also":
    She speaks French fluently. She also speaks Japanese.
  4. Join using "although":
    He worked very hard. He didn't get the promotion.
  5. Join using a relative clause with "whose":
    I admire the writer. Her novels have inspired millions.

Voir les réponses suggérées

  1. The man who is wearing a blue shirt is my uncle.
  2. If I had enough money, I would buy that car.
  3. She not only speaks French fluently but also Japanese.
  4. Although he worked very hard, he didn't get the promotion.
  5. I admire the writer whose novels have inspired millions.

Conseils pour l'Examen & Erreurs Fréquentes

Conseils pour l'examen

  • Identifiez d'abord le type de structure demandée avant de commencer à écrire.
  • Vérifiez la ponctuation, surtout pour les relatives non-définies (virgules nécessaires).
  • Dans les conditionnelles, faites attention à la concordance des temps.
  • Utilisez des variétés de structures pour montrer votre maîtrise de la langue.

Erreurs fréquentes

  • Confusion entre "which" et "who" - "who" pour les personnes, "which" pour les choses.
  • Oubli de la virgule dans les relatives non-définies: "My brother, who lives in Paris..."
  • Mauvaise concordance des temps dans les conditionnelles: "If I would have known..." au lieu de "If I had known..."
  • Utilisation incorrecte de "despite" sans "of": "Despite of the rain" au lieu de "Despite the rain".
  • Double négation avec "unless": "Unless you don't hurry" au lieu de "Unless you hurry".

Pour aller plus loin

Pour maîtriser les structures de phrases complexes, pratiquez régulièrement en:

  • Analysant des textes complexes et identifiant les différentes structures
  • Transformant des phrases simples en phrases complexes en utilisant différentes méthodes
  • Vous créant des flashcards avec les règles et formules importantes
  • Faisant des exercices chronométrés pour vous préparer aux conditions d'examen
